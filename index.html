Poverty is no joke.

United Nation'sSustainable Development Goal #1
This project aims to figure out what factors negatively affect a household's net income. By analyzing these factors, we hope to understand their relationship to poverty and inform strategies to reduce poverty rates in the Philippines to meet the target goal of reducing poverty to single-digit levels by 2028.

Overview

As of 2023, it is reported that 22.4% of the Philippines' population are living in conditions under the poverty line. A subset of this group are families that do not even have enough income to sustain themselves for their basic food needs. In concrete numbers, that's 9.79 million Filipinos who struggle to feed themselves. [1]

We want to do what we can in helping reduce the number of families who are living in unsustainable conditions, which is why we want to focus on identifying factors that negatively affect households so that they may be addressed.

Problem
The continuous struggle of Filipino households in sustaining their daily lives due to the prevalence of poverty in the Philippines.
Solution
Utilization of data science in identifying the factors that negatively affect the finances of Filipino households in hopes of inspiring government or community-driven actions.

Background

Over 25 million Filipinos are under poverty

Despite the government's interventions in addressing poverty, over 25.24 million Filipinos remain under the poverty line. Within this population, over 9.79 million of Filipinos are unable to meet their basic food needs. Moreover, around 7.2 million Filipinos are unable to escape poverty as they are trapped by the inflation rate in the country.

What are the factors that most negatively affect Filipino households' finances and increase risk of poverty?

Null Hypothesis
There is no significant correlation between household factors and financial well-being among families
Alternative Hypothesis
There is a significant negative correlation between household factors and financial well-being among families, indicating that higher expenses combined with low yearly income are associated with lower financial well-being and increased risk of poverty.
Action Plan
Analyze the different factors involved in households' expenditure in the dataset.

Data Collection

We contacted and accessed the Philippine Statistics Authority's (PSA) archive to gain access to data regarding the salary and wages of different households their expenses.

We will be using a sample of their latest dataset from the year 2021 (as of the time of writing) in analyzing the factors relating to Filipino poverty. We only used a sample of the data due to the huge data volume and the original data can be found here.
